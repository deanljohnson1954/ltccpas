State Mandated LTC

LTC State Tax

Like the long-term care tax that the state of Washington recently implemented, California and other states plan to introduce a tax on employees who do not own a long-term care insurance policy.

Act now before premiums rise. Contact us if you plan to obtain long-term care coverage. Premiums are likely to rise once state-mandated taxes are introduced, making the current time an ideal opportunity to purchase a policy.

California Long Term Care Task Force

The California Long Term Care Task Force was authorized by AB 567 and is charged with developing a long-term care program in California. Meeting notices, questionnaires, and related information can be accessed through the California Department of Insurance website.

Several other states are also actively evaluating mandated LTC programs similar to Washington's. If your state introduces a mandate, employees without qualifying LTC coverage may face payroll taxes that could have been avoided by purchasing a policy in advance.

Why This Matters to You

  • State-mandated programs typically offer limited benefit amounts — private policies provide superior coverage
  • Purchasing now locks in lower premiums based on your current age and health
  • Private LTC insurance can exempt you from state payroll taxes in states with mandates
  • Once a mandate takes effect, demand increases and premiums typically rise
