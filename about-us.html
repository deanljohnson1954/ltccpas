About Us — Meet Withbert W. Payne, CPA

Chartered Accountant, CPA, and independent LTC insurance broker with decades of experience in accounting and 25 years in LTC insurance

Withbert W. Payne, CPA

I am a CPA who applies audit-level financial analysis to life, long-term care, disability, and business insurance. Rather than selling policies, I independently audit them — to help you improve protection, reduce inefficiency, and make better financial decisions.

Insurance policies often drift out of alignment with changing financial goals. My role is to audit them objectively, identify inefficiencies, and recommend improvements before they become costly problems — the same discipline a CPA brings to a set of financial statements.

Why Independent Matters

To avoid a conflict of interest, your coverage should be evaluated by someone other than the agent who sold it. The selling agent has an inherent interest in defending the existing policy. An independent review has no such constraint.

  • •No carrier quotas
  • •No production requirements
  • •No obligation to defend an existing policy
  • •Recommendations based solely on your financial interests
  • •Analysis first. Recommendations second.

My Review Process

  1. Understand your goals.
  2. Audit your existing coverage.
  3. Compare appropriate market alternatives.
  4. Measure tax, cash-flow, and estate-planning impact.
  5. Deliver a written, no-obligation recommendation.

What I Review

  • •Life insurance — including chronic, critical, and long-term care riders, and ILIT / TOLI oversight
  • •Long-term care insurance — joint-life and hybrid designs with lifetime benefits
  • •Disability insurance — group versus individual coverage, and portability gaps
  • •Business coverage — buy-sell and key-person agreements
  • •Policy performance — restructuring, lapse prevention, and optimization

Why a CPA-Level Review

Your insurance should be evaluated the same way your CPA evaluates your financial statements — with independent analysis, objective recommendations, and no pressure to keep an outdated policy.

Every recommendation is weighed through tax treatment, cash-flow impact, estate and spousal-protection implications, hybrid-versus-standalone design, and — for business owners — premium deductibility. A well-structured policy can return more than it costs even if the benefit is never used.
