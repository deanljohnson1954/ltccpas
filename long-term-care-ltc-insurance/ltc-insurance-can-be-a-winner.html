LTC Insurance Can Be a Winner

Why the financial math — and the peace of mind — make LTC insurance a winning proposition in virtually every scenario.

Rethinking LTC Insurance as an Investment

Most people view LTC insurance through the lens of risk protection — which it is. But properly structured LTC insurance is also a financial instrument with compelling economics that can be analyzed and optimized like any investment. When you examine the numbers honestly, LTC insurance — particularly asset-based hybrid policies — wins in virtually every scenario.

The Financial Math: When LTC Is Used

Consider a 60-year-old who purchases a single-premium hybrid LTC policy for $350,000. The policy provides $24,000 per month in LTC benefits for both spouses, with lifetime coverage. If either spouse requires just 3 years of nursing home care at $24,000/month:

  • Benefits received: $24,000 × 36 months = $864,000
  • Net gain over premium: $514,000
  • ROI on LTC use alone: +147%

If care extends to 10 years — common for dementia — the numbers are even more dramatic:

  • Benefits received: $24,000 × 120 months = $2,880,000
  • Net gain over premium: $2,530,000
  • ROI on LTC use: +723%

This is why our OneAmerica case study showed a claim paying out more than 40× the total premiums paid.

When LTC Is Never Needed

With a traditional LTC policy, if care is never needed, the premiums are not returned — a legitimate financial concern. With a modern hybrid LTC policy, the story is completely different:

  • The $350,000 premium generates a guaranteed death benefit of approximately $400,000+ at the death of the second insured
  • Net gain to heirs: $50,000–$75,000 over the original premium
  • ROI if LTC is never used: +14% to +21%

The policyholder literally cannot lose. Either the policy funds expensive care needs, or it generates a death benefit exceeding the premium. There is no unfavorable outcome.

The Tax-Adjusted Return

The financial calculation becomes even more favorable when tax implications are considered. LTC benefits are generally received tax-free. For a client in a 40% combined federal and state tax bracket, receiving $864,000 in tax-free LTC benefits is the equivalent of receiving $1,440,000 in gross income. The after-tax value of LTC benefits is therefore dramatically higher than the nominal benefit amount suggests.

Comparing to the Best Alternatives

Where else could you invest $350,000 and expect:

  • Up to $2.8M in tax-free benefits if a serious care event occurs
  • A guaranteed return of your principal-plus at death if no claim is made
  • An asset on your balance sheet (immediate cash surrender value)
  • Full protection of your retirement savings from one of retirement's largest financial risks

The answer: virtually nowhere. The risk-adjusted, tax-adjusted, all-scenarios return on a well-structured hybrid LTC policy is exceptional relative to virtually any alternative use of those dollars.

Employer-Sponsored and Group LTC Options

For clients with access to group or employer-sponsored LTC insurance, these programs can offer additional advantages — including guaranteed issue (no medical underwriting required) for coverage up to specified limits, and potentially lower group rates. We help clients evaluate whether employer-sponsored LTC coverage is adequate, or whether supplemental private coverage is needed to fill gaps.

The Peace of Mind Factor

Beyond the financial returns, there is a dimension that cannot be captured in a spreadsheet: the peace of mind that comes from knowing your care is funded. Many of our clients tell us this is worth more than all the financial benefits combined. The ability to age without fear of burdening your family, to know your children won't face impossible choices, and to feel confident that quality care is assured regardless of what happens — this is the true value of LTC insurance done right.

The real question isn't whether you can afford LTC insurance. It's whether you can afford the alternative. One year in a Bay Area nursing home costs $288,000. One premium deposit funds decades of coverage and preserves everything else.
