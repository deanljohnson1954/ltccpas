Covered Long-Term Care Benefits

A long-term care insurance policy is designed to pay for a wide range of professional care services. The 12 benefit categories below represent the qualifying care settings and service types covered under most tax-qualified LTC policies. Coverage details, limits, and exclusions vary by policy — always review the Outline of Coverage provided by your licensed producer.

01

Informal Care

Care provided by family members or trusted friends in the home is one of the most used and valuable benefit categories.

02

Adult Day Care

Supervised daytime programs offering health, social, and therapeutic services — ideal for those living at home with a working caregiver.

03

Home Health Care

Professional skilled nursing, physical therapy, occupational therapy, and related services delivered directly in the home.

04

LTC Facility — All Levels

Coverage across all levels of a licensed long-term care facility — from custodial and intermediate care to full skilled nursing.

05

Assisted Living Facility

Room, board, and personal assistance services in a licensed assisted living community — the fastest-growing care setting in America.

06

Hospice Care

End-of-life comfort and palliative care services from a licensed hospice organization, provided in any qualifying setting.

07

Respite Care

Temporary relief for primary informal caregivers — allows family members to rest while the insured receives professional care.

08

Bed Reservation

Holds a nursing home or facility bed during a temporary hospitalization — preventing costly loss of placement in a care community.

09

International Facility

LTC benefits extend to qualifying care facilities outside the United States — providing global coverage for expatriates and frequent travelers.

10

Care Coordination

Professional assessment, planning, and coordination of care services — connecting the insured with the right providers and settings.

11

Caregiver Consultant

Access to a professional caregiver consultant who guides families through care decisions, transitions, and resource identification.

12

Supportive Equipment

Durable medical equipment and adaptive devices that help the insured remain safe and independent — at home or in a care facility.

Important

Benefit details, coverage limits, and exclusions vary by policy. Always review the Outline of Coverage provided by your licensed producer. Contact Withbert W. Payne, CPA, to discuss which benefit categories apply to your specific policy and situation.

What Initiates a Long-Term Care Claim?

Benefits are triggered when a physician certifies that the insured meets one of two qualifying standards — the ADL deficit standard or the cognitive impairment criteria — and the elimination period has been satisfied. The full benefit-trigger requirements, including physician certification and the elimination period, are detailed on the Claim Triggers page.
