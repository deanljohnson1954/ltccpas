Claim Triggers — What Initiates a Long-Term Care Claim?

The benefit eligibility standards that must be met before a long-term care insurance policy begins paying — and what every policyholder should understand before a claim arises.

A long-term care (LTC) insurance claim is triggered when a licensed healthcare professional certifies that a policyholder meets one or both of the following benefit eligibility standards. Understanding these standards before a claim arises ensures that families can respond quickly and access benefits without delay.

The Five Claim Triggers

01

ADL Deficit

The insured is unable to perform at least 2 of 6 activities of daily living — bathing, dressing, eating, toileting, transferring, and continence — without substantial assistance.

02

Cognitive Impairment

The insured has a severe cognitive condition, such as Alzheimer’s disease or another form of dementia, which requires substantial supervision to protect their health and safety.

03

Elimination Period

Most policies require a waiting period — typically 30 to 100 days — of qualifying care before benefits begin. This functions like a deductible and affects how quickly coverage kicks in.

04

Physician Certification

A licensed healthcare practitioner must certify that the impairment is expected to last at least 90 days — a requirement under HIPAA for tax-qualified LTC policies.

05

Care Plan Established

A plan of care prescribed by a licensed professional must be in place, specifying the type and frequency of care needed by the insured.

Please Note

Policies vary. Always review your specific contract for benefit triggers, definitions of “substantial assistance,” and any inflation or indemnity provisions. Contact your insurance carrier or a licensed LTC specialist to begin the claims process.
