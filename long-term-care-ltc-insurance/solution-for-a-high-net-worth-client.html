LTC Solutions for High Net Worth Clients

Why wealthy clients need LTC planning — and the sophisticated strategies that protect and grow their estates.

The Misconception: "I'm Wealthy Enough to Self-Insure"

High net worth individuals frequently assume they don't need LTC insurance — that their assets are sufficient to cover any care costs that arise. This assumption, while understandable, overlooks several critical realities that our CPA team identifies in virtually every high net worth client review.

The truth is that wealthy clients have the most to lose from an uninsured LTC event — and often the most to gain from a properly structured LTC policy. LTC insurance for affluent clients is less about affordability and more about efficiency, asset protection, and legacy planning.

Why Wealthy Clients Need LTC Planning

The Costs Can Still Be Significant

Even a $5–10 million net worth can be meaningfully impacted by LTC costs. A 10-year dementia episode in a premium memory care facility in the Bay Area could cost $2–3 million. That's 20%–60% of a $5 million estate — a substantial erosion of the wealth you've spent decades building.

Quality of Care Matters More

Wealthy clients typically have higher expectations for the quality and setting of their care — private rooms, specialized facilities, around-the-clock in-home care. These premium care options cost significantly more than standard Medicaid-level care. Without LTC insurance, even affluent clients may find quality care options more constrained than expected during a prolonged care episode.

Estate Preservation and Legacy

For clients focused on leaving a legacy to children, grandchildren, or charitable causes, LTC insurance is an estate preservation tool. Every dollar paid by an LTC policy to cover care is a dollar that stays in the estate. A $3 million LTC event that is fully covered by insurance is a $3 million gift to your heirs.

Strategies for High Net Worth Clients

Large Benefit Pool Policies

High net worth clients should consider policies with large benefit pools — $2M to $5M or more in total available LTC benefits. These can be structured as traditional policies with inflation protection or as asset-based policies funded by a larger single-premium deposit.

Shared Care Riders for Couples

For married couples, shared care riders are particularly valuable. These provisions allow one spouse to draw on the other's unused benefit pool if their own benefits are exhausted. For a couple with $2M in combined policy benefits, each can draw on the full $2M pool — rather than being limited to their individual $1M allocation.

Return-of-Premium Options

Some hybrid LTC policies offer a return-of-premium rider — guaranteeing that if the policy is surrendered at death, the heirs receive at least the total premiums paid. This further eliminates any possibility of a "losing" outcome from the insurance purchase.

Corporate-Owned LTC Insurance for Business Owners

For clients who own C corporations, LTC insurance premiums paid by the corporation may be fully deductible as a business expense — with benefits received by the insured remaining tax-free. This creates a powerful tax-advantaged LTC funding mechanism for business owners and executives. Our CPA background makes us uniquely qualified to help clients structure this correctly.

ILIT Integration

For clients with Irrevocable Life Insurance Trusts (ILITs), certain hybrid LTC-life insurance products can be held inside the trust structure, combining estate tax planning with LTC protection in a single integrated instrument.

Tax Deductibility of LTC Premiums

LTC insurance premiums are deductible as a medical expense on Schedule A, subject to age-based limits set by the IRS. For clients aged 70 and older, the eligible deduction can be up to $6,000+ per person per year (amounts indexed annually). For business owners using corporate payment structures, the deduction can be even more substantial.

Additionally, benefits received from a qualified LTC policy are generally tax-free, regardless of income level. This makes LTC insurance one of the few insurance products where both the premium deduction and the benefit payment create a tax advantage.

The Legacy Protection Perspective

We often present LTC insurance to high net worth clients through a legacy lens: the policy premium is the cost of guaranteeing that your estate passes to your heirs — rather than to a nursing home or memory care facility. Given that a single LTC event can cost $1–3 million, the premium of a well-structured LTC policy is often the best estate planning dollar a wealthy client can spend.
