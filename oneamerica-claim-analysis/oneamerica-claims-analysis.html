OneAmerica Asset-Based LTC Policy — A Policy Analysis

How a $360,000 single premium provides lifetime care coverage for both insureds — and why the net cost of that protection is $60,000.

$360,000 Single Premium
$30,000 Monthly Benefit — Both Insureds
$300,000 Guaranteed Death Benefit

The Policy in Plain Terms

A single premium of $360,000 funds lifetime long-term care coverage for both insureds. The current cost of nursing home care for a couple is $30,000 per month.

The policy addresses both possible outcomes — care needed, or care never needed — and limits the true net cost of securing lifetime care protection to $60,000.

"One payment. Lifetime protection for both of you. And if care is never needed, your family receives the money back as a guaranteed death benefit."

If Care Is Needed

The policy pays $30,000 per month for as long as care is required — with no lifetime cap.

In a 10-year dementia scenario — or an accident scenario of similar duration — that represents $3,600,000 in benefits ($30,000 × 120 months). The policy continues to pay regardless of how long care is needed beyond that.

With the OneAmerica Policy
  • $30,000/month paid — no benefit cap
  • $3,600,000 in a 10-year scenario
  • Family assets fully preserved
  • No financial burden placed on children
  • Quality care in chosen facility
  • Coverage applies to both insureds
Self-Insuring
  • $30,000/month out-of-pocket
  • $360,000 depleted within 12 months
  • Assets beyond that must be liquidated
  • Limited to Medicaid-eligible facilities
  • $3,600,000 total exposure at 10 years
  • Estate partially or fully eliminated

If Care Is Never Needed

The policy pays a $300,000 death benefit to the named beneficiary. The original $360,000 premium is not forfeited — $300,000 is returned to the estate regardless of whether care was ever needed.

The Net Cost of Coverage

The net cost of securing lifetime care protection — after accounting for the guaranteed death benefit — is $60,000.

$360,000 premium − $300,000 guaranteed death benefit = $60,000 net cost for lifetime long-term care coverage for both insureds.

The Self-Insurance Comparison

Self-insuring means retaining the $360,000 and paying $30,000 per month out of pocket if care becomes necessary. The $360,000 covers approximately 12 months of care. In a 10-year dementia or accident scenario, the total out-of-pocket exposure is $3,600,000 — drawn entirely from personal and family assets.

Self-insuring is not a strategy — it is an unquantified exposure. The policy converts that exposure into a defined, $60,000 net cost.

The Tax Dimension

LTC benefits paid under a qualified policy are received tax-free. To fund $3,600,000 in care costs from after-tax assets at a combined federal and state tax rate of 40%, a family would need to have accumulated approximately $6,000,000 in gross assets — just to cover one extended care scenario. The policy delivers equivalent protection for a net cost of $60,000.

Key Takeaways

  • A single premium of $360,000 provides lifetime LTC coverage for both insureds — no benefit cap
  • The $30,000 monthly benefit matches the current cost of nursing home care for a couple
  • A 10-year dementia or accident scenario produces $3,600,000 in tax-free benefits
  • If care is never needed, $300,000 is returned to the named beneficiary as a guaranteed death benefit
  • The net cost of coverage — after the death benefit — is $60,000
  • Self-insuring exposes the family to $3,600,000 in potential out-of-pocket costs
