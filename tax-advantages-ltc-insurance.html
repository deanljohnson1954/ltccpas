The Tax Advantages of LTC Insurance

Federal and California tax treatment of long-term care insurance — C-Corp deductibility, individual premium limits, the Partnership Program, and a real cost example.

Long-term care insurance is among the most tax-favored financial products available — but the size of the benefit depends heavily on how the premium is paid and which entity pays it. This guide summarizes the federal and California tax treatment of qualifying LTC coverage for individuals, the self-employed, and business owners, with the 2026 figures.

100% C-Corp Deduction on LTC Portion

No age-based limits — the most tax-efficient structure for funding LTC insurance.

$6,200 Max Individual Eligible Premium

Age 71+ (2026). Age-based limits apply to federally qualified policy premiums.

~$68K True Net Cost — CA C-Corp Couple

Illustrative: age-50 couple, single-premium policy, after C-Corp tax savings of ~$30,253.

100% Benefits Received Tax-Free

LTC insurance benefits paid to the insured are generally received income-tax-free.

Federal Deductibility of LTC Premiums

Long-term care insurance premiums on a federally qualified policy may be deductible as a medical expense on your federal return, subject to age-based limits and — for itemizers — the 7.5% AGI floor. The limits below are per insured person, using attained age at the end of the tax year (IRS Rev. Proc. 2025-32).

Self-Employed Deduction

If you are self-employed (sole proprietor, partner, or S-Corp shareholder owning more than 2%), you may deduct eligible LTC premiums as an above-the-line deduction — not subject to the 7.5% AGI floor — up to the age-based limits above (claimed via Form 7206).

C-Corporation — Maximum Tax Efficiency

A C-Corporation can deduct 100% of the LTC care portion of a qualifying hybrid policy premium paid for employee-owners and their spouses — with no age-based limits. This is the most tax-efficient structure for funding long-term care insurance.

Figures above are an illustrative example for one policy design. Actual premiums are carrier-filed and individually underwritten.

California State Partnership Program

California participates in the federal Long-Term Care Partnership Program, which allows policyholders with a qualifying Partnership-certified LTC policy to protect assets equal to the benefits paid out — dollar for dollar — when applying for Medi-Cal. In effect, every dollar of qualifying LTC coverage does double duty: it pays for care and shields an equal amount of assets.

  • Policy must be California Partnership-certified at the time of purchase
  • Dollar-for-dollar Medi-Cal asset protection matching benefits paid
  • Reciprocal — protection recognized in other participating states
  • Inflation protection required for purchasers under age 76
  • California follows federal qualified LTC policy definitions
2026 Note

Per the California Department of Health Care Services, the carriers historically certified for the California Partnership are not currently selling new Partnership-certified policies. Separately, most Non-MAGI Medi-Cal asset limits were reinstated January 1, 2026 — which makes the Partnership’s asset protection meaningful again for existing policyholders. Confirm current Partnership availability before relying on certification as a purchase criterion.
