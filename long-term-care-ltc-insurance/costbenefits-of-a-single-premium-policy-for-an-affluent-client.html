Cost/Benefits of a Single Premium Policy for an Affluent Client

How one strategic deposit can generate millions in LTC coverage — with compelling tax advantages.

The Single Premium Advantage

For clients who have accumulated liquid assets — whether in savings accounts, CDs, low-yield investments, or existing life insurance and annuity policies — a single-premium asset-based LTC policy represents one of the most efficient financial instruments available. With one strategic deposit, you can generate LTC coverage that is 3×–5× the amount invested, eliminate the "use it or lose it" problem, simplify your financial structure, and in many cases achieve this using tax-deferred or tax-free dollars.

How the Math Works: Example Scenarios

Scenario A: $100,000 Single Premium

A 60-year-old in good health deposits $100,000 into a single-premium hybrid LTC policy. Typical structure:

  • LTC coverage pool: $300,000–$400,000
  • Monthly LTC benefit: $8,000–$10,000 per month
  • Guaranteed death benefit if LTC never used: $115,000–$130,000
  • Immediate cash surrender value: typically 85%–95% of premium in year one

Scenario B: $350,000 Single Premium (Couple)

This closely mirrors our Professional Couple case study: a joint policy for two insureds with a combined $346,000 premium provides $24,000/month in combined LTC benefits (lifetime), with a death benefit of $400,000 paid upon the second insured's death. The effective net cost — after accounting for the immediate cash surrender value of $118,916 — is approximately $227,000 for lifetime joint coverage.

Scenario C: $400,000 Single Premium Annuity (Single)

This mirrors our Rich Widow case study: a single-premium annuity with LTC rider provides $16,667/month for up to 6 years, totaling up to $1.2M in LTC benefits. If care is never needed, the annuity value continues to grow and can be surrendered or inherited.

The Tax Advantages of Single Premium LTC

1035 Exchange — Tax-Free Asset Repositioning

One of the most powerful and underutilized strategies in LTC planning is the IRC Section 1035 exchange. This provision allows you to transfer the cash value of an existing life insurance policy or annuity directly into a new LTC-funded policy — completely tax-free. This is especially valuable for clients who hold older life insurance policies or non-qualified annuities that have accumulated significant gains, as those gains would normally be taxable upon surrender.

LTC Benefits Are Tax-Free

Benefits received from a qualified LTC insurance policy are generally not considered taxable income. This means the LTC benefits — which can reach millions of dollars over a long claim — are received free of federal income tax, making the effective value even greater than the dollar amount suggests.

Tax-Free Death Benefit

If LTC is never needed, the death benefit paid to heirs from a life insurance-based hybrid policy is also generally income-tax-free to the beneficiaries, providing an efficient wealth transfer mechanism in addition to LTC protection.

Comparison to Self-Insuring

Consider what self-insuring the same LTC risk actually requires. To fund $1.2 million in potential LTC costs from after-tax savings at a 40% combined tax rate, you would need to earn $2 million in gross income — just to cover that one risk. And that's assuming the money is never needed for anything else, never subject to market losses, and the timing is perfectly predictable. With a single-premium LTC policy, a $400,000 deposit generates the $1.2M in coverage — a far more efficient use of capital.

Who Is the Ideal Candidate?

  • Clients aged 55–75 in good health with $100,000 or more in liquid or repositionable assets
  • Those currently self-insuring who recognize the risk but haven't acted yet
  • Clients with existing life insurance or annuity cash values eligible for a 1035 exchange
  • Those who want simplicity — one payment, no ongoing premium obligation
  • Clients focused on estate efficiency who want every dollar to serve a purpose
  • Anyone who finds ongoing annual premiums uncomfortable or administratively burdensome

Cash Surrender Value — Your Money Is Not Locked Away

One of the most important features of single-premium asset-based LTC policies is that your money retains meaningful liquidity. Unlike term life insurance (which has no cash value), single-premium hybrid policies typically have a cash surrender value of 85%–95% of the premium in year one, growing over time. This means if your circumstances change and you decide you no longer want the policy, you can surrender it and recover most of your principal. Your money is not locked away.
