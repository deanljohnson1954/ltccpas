Sample Long-Term Care Planning Illustrations

Illustrative examples for 50-year-old couples at different planning levels. Actual premiums and benefits vary by age, health, underwriting, and policy design.

Four Levels, One Guaranteed Structure

These sample illustrations demonstrate different planning objectives — the same guaranteed structure funded at four levels, from mainstream coverage to concierge protection. Read them as a ladder: each step adds room above tomorrow’s cost of care.

  • $15,000 monthly benefit (per insured): designed to help cover the cost of a semiprivate room.
  • $20,000 monthly benefit (per insured): designed for partners at the Big 4 and other professionals seeking protection at private-room cost levels.
  • $40,000 monthly benefit (per insured): concierge-level planning for affluent and high-net-worth families who want the highest level of long-term care protection.
  • $40,000 monthly benefit with 3% compound inflation: designed for families who want concierge-level protection that grows with future long-term care costs.

A Note for Business Owners

At every level, a portion of the premium is the long-term care premium. Depending on entity structure, that portion may be deductible under IRS guidelines — and long-term care benefits paid under a qualified policy are generally received income-tax-free. Tax treatment depends on your facts; consult your own CPA.
